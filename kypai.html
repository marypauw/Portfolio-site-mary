KYP.ai

KYP.ai is a process mining and analytic tool that gives you an overview of your processes and provides you with insightful data and recommendation on how to better your processes through RPA, IPA and AI. My role as Graphic and UX designer is very connected to the mission of providing a clear and easy way to visualise data and experience a smooth navigation through a rather complicated software.

As UX UI designer am also involved in the ideation of new features with the product team from the phase of discovery and the realisation of the documentation until the final QA process, managing the relationship with developers and ensuring that the desing language correspond to the development language, and that the programming process respect the business needs and times.

01

The UI Brush-up

I fell in love with the idea of the KYP.ai product, the complexity, and how these innovative ideas could help companies to better their processes. I've also had the opportunity to learn more about business, automation,and AI. Being surrounded by such a visionary and innovative team with a solid background in business consulting and new development technologies had been inspiring.

One of the first projects that I approached was how to better a product with so much potential so that it would be easily understandable from every memeber of the organization and that could offer the best user experience and navigation. I decided to approach the project with a “revolution” collaborating with the product and development team.

Create a new navigation
The old navigation had a confusing and incongruent style. Following the business idea of concentrating the whole product on the concepts SEE, UNDERSTAND, CHANGE, I designed a navigation with a search engine and the possibility to create your dashboard by opening new panels from the left menu. A new style was given to the whole navigation and more visual elements will now help the users to understand which part of the process they wish to analyze.

Improve the layout
Another aspect of the product that needed a revolution was the general layout of the dashboard. Too much space was being unnecessarily used and which created problems or frustration in terms of usability on smaller laptops, which would have been a general screen size for our users. I decided to decrease the height of the automation bar and review alignment, layout, and padding inside the panels which created general guidelines for future development.

Improve the color scheme
In the meantime, another goal in this phase was to create a new color scheme to communicate to the user not only the professional but also the emotional value of the data that they were going to visualize. I created a new palette for the automation bar to help the user decern between all the different data types which resulted in cleaner, more understandable component.

 
02

Design phases and styleguide

KYP.ai works with an agile scrum system. There are also several phases to get from a feature idea to production. This allows the team to be focused and deliver fast.

Phase 1 IDEATION
Ideation workshops, discussion of new ideas and projects, initial low fidelity drawings of the new feature.

Phase 2 DEFINE
First mid-fidelity mockups and discussion with the product owner and business analyst regarding the value of the feature.   Afterwards we have a discussion with the production/development team about possible blockers and how we can develop the new feature, and what needs to be changed in the design.

Phase 3 PRODUCTION
High-fidelity mockups and prototype in alignment with the KYP.ai style guide. First development phase in which I attend several meetings with the developers to review the design even in the phase of production.

Phase 4 QA
Once developed the new feature will be reviewed by the QA and testing team. I am always involved in this phase to check if and how the design works inside the product and its responsiveness. Most of the time this is a great opportunity for me to see how the product and the design system can be improved.

Phase 5 USER and TESTING
Since KYP.ai is still a product its early stages, we have had the opportunity to gather feedback from potential customers already in a very fresh phase of its development. When a new feature is created its realesed into a demo environment and presented by the sales team to the test customers. This is also a very important stage to understand the improvement potential of the product. In this phase, I'm involved in creating test methods and user research analysis such as creating user personas, user journeys, and usability testing.

The styleguide
After the phase of the revolution, a very important thing to establish was the KYP.ai style guide for marketing and product development purposes. I strongly believe that designers and developers should reach the point in which they “speak the same language”, and for this reason, I decided to develop a style guide strictly related to the way frontend developers use classes for CSS and angular components. I created a style guide in our shared documentation Cloud, connected to Figma, where the naming of UI elements and color would be the same used in development for classes, variables, and components, often aligning with the developers to find the right structure for it.

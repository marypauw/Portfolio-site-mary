Fitogram

Fitogram is a software for gym management available in6 different languages for any kind of device.

At Fitogram I have the opportunity of being mentored by a senior UI designer to create clean and light designs,in a team with a senior and junior UX designers. The team of Fitogram collaborate with an agile work system in oder to create a useful and easy product, despite the complexity of the software.

My role is supporting the design team but also the marketing team with some graphic design tasks, all the final designs have been ideated and finally reviewed by Franziska Veh, Senior UI designer.

01

Website assets and product mockups

One of the tasks that I was assigned to at Fitogram was to support for the redesign and relaunch of the website. I had my first experiences trying to desing layouts for the desktop and the mobile view. Lately in the project I was assigned for the creation of the single assets for desktop and mobile in every language, based on existing designs of the product.The goal was to show the different functionalities of the software and the latests designs of the booking tool.

see Fitogram Website
 
02

Resposive onboarding

The Fitogram team decided to ideate and design an onboarding process, to explain to the providers the first steps into learning how to use the software, namely creating a customer, an event and a product to sell. After realising the onboarding process for desktop, the product manager realised that a lot of user were going through the process also from their mobile, so we needed a new design that could be adapted and usable also from a mobile device. I created the experience for a mobile view with the mentoring of our UX and UI designer, adapting the existing designs (made by Franziska Veh) to the new need.

 
03

FitoApp Dark Mode

In 2019 Fitogram launched a mobile application where end customers could book classes through the booking tool. To adapt the designs to the latest needs of the market, I was in charge of creating a dark mode version of the existing designs of the FitoApp (made by Franziska Veh) and this was a great opputunity for me to do further researches in the dark mode world and IOs Guidelines. The greatest challenge for me was how to adapt our brand blue to a black background, which brought me to dive deeper into contrast ratio articles regarding dark mode designs.

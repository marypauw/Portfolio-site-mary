Fitogram

Fitogram is a studio management software available in several different languages for any kind of device.

At Fitogram I had the opportunity to be mentored by a senior UI designer to create clean and elegant designs. The team at Fitogram collaborates with an agile work system in order to create an intuitive and user-friendly product, even despite the complexity the software sometimes presents itself with.

My role was supporting the design team but also the marketing team with some graphic design tasks. All the final designs have been ideated and finally reviewed by Franziska Veh, Senior UI designer.

Projects

 
01

Website assets and product mockups

One of the tasks that I was assigned to at Fitogram was to support the redesign and relaunch of the website. I developed new layouts for the desktop and the mobile view. Later in the project I was assigned to the creation of single assets for desktop and mobile in every language. The goal was to show the different functionalities of the software and the latests designs of the booking tool.

see Fitogram Website
 
02

Responsive onboarding

The Fitogram team decided to ideate and design an onboarding process to explain to the providers the first steps into learning how to use the software. This entailed creating a customer, an event and a product to sell. After creating the onboarding process for desktop, the product manager realised that a lot of user were going through the process also from their mobile, so we needed a new design that could be adapted and usable also from a mobile device. I created the experience for a mobile view with the mentoring of our UX and UI designer, adapting the existing designs to the new views.

 
03

FitoApp Dark Mode

In 2019 Fitogram launched a mobile application where end customers could book classes through the booking tool. To adapt the designs to the latest needs of the market, I was in charge of creating a dark mode version of the existing designs of the FitoApp which was a great opportunity for me to research the delicate balances of the dark mode world and the IOs Guidelines. The greatest challenge for me was how to adapt our brand blue to a black background, which brought me to dive deeper into contrast ratio articles regarding dark mode designs.
